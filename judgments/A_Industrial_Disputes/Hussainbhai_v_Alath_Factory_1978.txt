IN THE HIGH COURT OF KERALA AT ERNAKULAM WP(C).No. 35902 of 2009(G) 1. SIBGA ARTS AND SCIENCE COLLEGE, ... Petitioner Vs 1. THE STATE OF KERALA, ... Respondent 2. KANNUR UNIVERSITY, For Petitioner :SRI.C.KHALID For Respondent :SRI.M.SASEENDRAN,SC,KANNUR UNIVERSITY The Hon'ble MR. Justice T.R.RAMACHANDRAN NAIR Dated :11/10/2010 O R D E R T.R. RAMACHANDRAN NAIR, J. ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~ W.P.(C). No.35902/2009-G ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~ Dated this the 11th day of October, 2010 J U D G M E N T

The petitioner herein is aggrieved by the denial of affiliation for M.Com course as per Ext.P6.

2. The petitioner is a Self Financing College in Kannur District run by a charitable trust represented by its Manager. The college was started on 27/07/2005 with the courses B.Sc Physics with Computer Science and Chemistry, B.Sc Chemistry with Physics and Computer Science and B.Com with Computer Applications. The college is affiliated to the second respondent-University.

3. In the academic year 2008, the petitioner applied for getting additional affiliation for four courses, namely, BBM; BCA (with Technical English); B.A (English) with English Fiction & Journalism (Sub) and; M.Com. Ext.P2 is the true copy of the application. An inspection Commission was appointed and, finally, by Ext.P6 all the courses except M.Com have been granted affiliation. The application was disposed of in the light of the directions issued in W.P.(C).No.27430/2008. The petitioner was constrained to file C.O.C.No.354/2009 which was disposed of by Ext.P7 wherein this Court directed the University to W.P.(C). No.35902/2009 -:2:- furnish a report of the Inspection Commission on application.

4. It appears that the defect to be rectified was regarding the books and computers which according to the petitioner have already been made available and Exts.P9 to P13 documents have been produced in support of the above contention. It is pointed out in the writ petition that even though the University informed that an inspection team will be sent, that did not materialise.

5. The University has filed a counter affidavit and the petitioner has filed a reply affidavit also.

6. Heard both sides. The learned Standing Counsel for the University submitted that the petitioner had to furnish Financial Guarantee and rectify the defects pointed out by the Inspection Committee. The learned counsel for the petitioner submitted that the Financial Guarantee has already been furnished as evident from Ext.P19 in April 2010. The same was furnished after filing of the writ petition and the learned Standing Counsel for the second respondent submitted that they have received the Financial Guarantee.

7. Therefore, what remains is the conduct of inspection by an Inspection Commission to assess the W.P.(C). No.35902/2009 -:3:- facilities. The learned Standing Counsel for the University also submitted that the petitioner may have to submit a further application seeking affiliation. Therefore, the petitioner will file an application in continuation of the application already submitted as per Ext.P2 within a period of three weeks from today and, thereafter the University will take steps to conduct an inspection by a duly appointed Inspection Commission. Further action in this regard will be finalised within a further period of two months. All efforts will be taken to complete the other formalities within a stipulated time. The writ petition is accordingly disposed of. No costs.

(T.R. Ramachandran Nair, Judge.) ms
